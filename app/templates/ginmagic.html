Gin, Magic, Music, and Spirit

by GP

Posted on February 9th, 2015 at 11:00 PM

I love reading blogs from other startup guys, cause if they are crazy enough to be working on maintaining a blog while they crank out 80+ hour work weeks you get a unique sight – a raw and passionate perspective on life from some really interesting individuals. Now granted a lot of these end up being really technical or bit out there – but I feel its worth it when you find that one article where you think to yourself “Damn I gotta meet this dude (or lady)”. Usually that is due to how passionate the guy is, but the thing that got me to write this article is not passion but Gin

Not to long ago I was reading a blog post from a cofounder who was in the Tech Stars program and he was talking about his experience which to be honest I don’t remember much – except one line where he said something to the effect “its 14 hour days, and its stress and you just gotta cope with it; everyone does in a different way – my preference is Gin.”

To be honest my first thought to this was ‘wow I haven’t heard someone be so honest with a drinking coping mechanism since Vandy.’ My next thought was ‘Really Gin? What’s wrong with this guy? Who legitimately likes Gin unless they’re trying to impress classy women – But… on a more serious note I appreciate the honesty and he’s not alone. Now I’m not writing this article to rag on this guy, I’m writing this article to actually talk about a good friend of mine who I respect a lot – and how I admire how he copes with stress.

The individual I am talking about (let’s call him TaCo) is at Vandy med and the kid gets 6 hours of sleep a night all 7 days a week (if he’s lucky) and maybe 4-8 hours of free-time a week if he schedules it right. Between the schedule and the overall med school thing you can imagine this kid has got a lot of stress on his plate, but he copes with it in a way which I can only describe as part admiration and part envy (and part I try to emulate).

So what’s his secret, to handling stress?

Well it’s Magic.

Seriously the kid just picked up the hobby and became obsessed with it. He practices in his free time, with his friends, and I even bet with his patients. He has a knack for finding the joy in an activity versus being overwhelmed by its frustrating moments.

It’s also Music.

We all live in Nashville, so we have a pretty amazing music scene here. But TaCo – and his whole family for that matter – LOVE music (even by Nashville standards). TaCo in particular loves folksy Blue Grass, and he is quite good at the bongo, guitar, harmonica and (I think) the mandolin and he jams all the time. Besides deriving joy from playing himself, he also loves just listening to music. So I would say the takeaway here is the appreciation of art for the sake that it brings joy – which I would argue is harder said than done because we so easily try to make art about ourselves than the joy it provides.

And the most important, Spirit.

I know this point may be a bit more controversial than the previous points, but I can also say as a direct witness – this is the most impactful aspect to dealing with stress. TaCo has a really solid relationship with Christ and it is clearly played out in his life. Whether it comes from reading his bible in the morning, singing worship music in his room before he goes to bed, or prayer throughout the day; it is clear that his relationship with Jesus gives him peace. It is so overwhelmingly clear because that is exactly what it is – it’s an overwhelming and contagious peace that I am the benefactor of simple by proximity. And it is a peace that can conquer any stress.

Don’t worry about anything; instead, pray about everything. Tell God what you need, and thank him for all he has done. Then you will experience God’s peace, which exceeds anything we can understand.

Philippians 4:6-7

--Mazzone
